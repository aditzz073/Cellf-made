# Cellf-made

> **Early Sepsis Detection Through AI-Powered Immune Transcriptomic Analysis**

---

## Problem Statement

Sepsis is a life-threatening medical emergency caused by a dysregulated immune response to infection. Despite being one of the leading causes of ICU mortality worldwide, it is typically diagnosed only after observable signs of organ dysfunction or abnormal white blood cell counts emerge — by which point the window for effective intervention has often already narrowed.

This late-stage detection model drives high mortality rates, prolonged ICU stays, and significant healthcare costs.

---

## Solution

**Cellf-made** leverages AI and immune transcriptomics to detect sepsis at the molecular level — before clinical symptoms manifest.

By analyzing gene expression patterns in patient immune cells, the system identifies the subtle biological signals that precede visible illness, giving clinicians an early warning that traditional diagnostics cannot provide.

---

## How It Works

1. **Sample Collection** — A blood sample is drawn from the patient.
2. **Transcriptomic Profiling** — Immune cell gene expression is measured to capture the molecular state of the patient's immune response.
3. **AI-Driven Analysis** — A machine learning model trained on large-scale transcriptomic datasets identifies sepsis-specific expression patterns characteristic of early-stage disease.
4. **Risk Stratification** — The system generates a quantitative risk score or real-time alert, delivered directly to the clinical team.

---

## What Makes This Different

Most sepsis diagnostics rely on downstream clinical indicators — fever, hypotension, elevated lactate — that only appear once the cascade has already begun. Cellf-made moves the detection window upstream by targeting the molecular changes that *drive* that cascade, not the symptoms it produces.

This shift from **symptom-based diagnosis** to **molecular-level prediction** is the core innovation.

---

## Impact

| Outcome | Effect |
|---|---|
| Earlier intervention | Antibiotics and fluids administered before organ stress begins |
| Reduced organ failure | Catching the immune response early limits downstream damage |
| Lower mortality | Faster, more targeted treatment improves survival rates |
| Shorter ICU stays | Early management reduces disease severity and length of admission |
| Healthcare cost savings | Fewer complications mean fewer resources spent on late-stage care |
